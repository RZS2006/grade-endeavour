Tutorial

1. Add Courses

To begin, input the course name and course grade goal, and add it. A course grade goal can be valid grade between 4 to 10, or 1 to 7 for IB students. After clicking the submit button, the application will create a collapsible dropdown for that course. This shows the three important numbers that you need to know: your current average, your aimed average, and your needed average.

2. Add Course Entries

For the application to function, you must also input course entries. To add course entries, input the name, type, date, and grade of the course entry. The course entry type can be, for example, an exam or an essay. The course entry grade must also be a valid grade depending on the course's grading scale. After adding the course entry, the app will automatically calculate you averages. It lists all your course entries in chronological order.

3. Endeavour!

All you have to do now is glimpse at your needed average to see what you need to effortlessly practice more. You can also edit your course goal through the forecast span and tolerance. By increasing forecast span, the app calculate the needed average of you next n course entries, so you spread out your effort. By increasing tolerance, you temporarily adjust you course goal to something tolerable to make it easier to achieve it. For example, tolerating 8.80 from 9.00 can dampen your required effort.
